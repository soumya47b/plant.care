Kalanchoe

Introduction

Kalanchoes are succulent plants known for their attractive, long-lasting blooms and fleshy leaves. They are popular as houseplants and in outdoor gardens in warmer climates.

They thrive with minimal care and can tolerate dry conditions.

How to Grow

  1. Plant in well-draining soil with bright, indirect sunlight.
  2. Water sparingly, allowing the soil to dry out between waterings.
  3. Fertilize with a balanced, water-soluble fertilizer every 4-6 weeks during the growing season.
  4. Ensure good air circulation to avoid fungal issues.
  5. Protect from frost and extreme cold temperatures.
